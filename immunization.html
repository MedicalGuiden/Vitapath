Why Childhood Immunization Matters

Vaccination, also known as immunization, is one of the most effective ways to protect children from life-threatening diseases. It involves giving a child a small, safe amount of a weakened or inactive form of a virus or bacteria, which helps their immune system recognize and fight the disease in the future.

Why Is It Important?

  • Prevents Serious Illness: Protects against deadly diseases like measles, polio, TB, and hepatitis.
  • Builds Immunity Early: The earlier a child is vaccinated, the sooner they are protected.
  • Protects Others: Helps create community immunity (herd immunity).
  • Cost-Effective: Preventing is cheaper than treating diseases.

When Should a Child Be Vaccinated?

Vaccines are given according to a schedule starting at birth:

  • At Birth: BCG, Oral Polio
  • 2, 4, 6 Months: DTP, Hepatitis B, Polio, Hib, Rotavirus
  • 9 Months: Measles
  • 18 Months: Booster doses

Each country may vary, but the principle is the same: vaccinate early and on time.
